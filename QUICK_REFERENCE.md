## NEW STUDIES (7 Total)

### 1. araujo-2019
- **Title**: Prevalence of Optimal Metabolic Health in American Adults
- **Authors**: Araújo, J., Cai, J., Stevens, J.
- **Year**: 2019 | **PMID**: 30484738 | **DOI**: 10.1089/met.2018.0105
- **Journal**: Metabolic Syndrome and Related Disorders
- **Sample**: 16,914 US adults (NHANES 2009-2016)
- **Study Type**: Cross-sectional
- **Key Finding**: Only 12.2% of Americans are metabolically healthy
- **QS**: 11
- **Primary Card**: q-means-framework

---

### 2. mottillo-2010
- **Title**: The Metabolic Syndrome and Cardiovascular Risk
- **Authors**: Mottillo, S., Filion, K.B., Genest, J., et al.
- **Year**: 2010 | **PMID**: 20863953 | **DOI**: 10.1016/j.jacc.2010.05.034
- **Journal**: Journal of the American College of Cardiology
- **Sample**: 951,083 (meta-analysis of 87 studies)
- **Study Type**: Meta-analysis
- **Key Finding**: MetS → 2-fold CVD risk, 1.5-fold mortality
- **QS**: 12
- **Primary Card**: q-score-low

---

### 3. kramer-2013
- **Title**: Are Metabolically Healthy Overweight and Obesity Benign?
- **Authors**: Kramer, C.K., Zinman, B., Retnakaran, R.
- **Year**: 2013 | **PMID**: 24297192 | **DOI**: 10.7326/0003-4819-159-11-201312030-00008
- **Journal**: Annals of Internal Medicine
- **Sample**: 61,386 (8 prospective cohorts)
- **Study Type**: Meta-analysis
- **Key Finding**: MHO → 1.24× CVD/mortality vs normal weight
- **QS**: 11
- **Primary Card**: q-score-high (secondary)
- **Related Card**: q-improve-metabolic

---

### 4. malmö-2003
- **Title**: Hyperinsulinemia as long-term predictor of death
- **Authors**: Ljungman, S., Wikstrand, J., Hartford, M., et al.
- **Year**: 2003 | **PMID**: 12542553 | **DOI**: 10.1046/j.1365-2796.2003.01077.x
- **Journal**: Journal of Internal Medicine
- **Sample**: 6,074 Swedish men (19-year follow-up)
- **Study Type**: Prospective cohort
- **Key Finding**: Hyperinsulinemia → 1.58× all-cause mortality
- **QS**: 9
- **Primary Card**: q-insulin (second study)

---

### 5. karelis-2007
- **Title**: Relationship Between Low-Grade Inflammation and Insulin Resistance
- **Authors**: Karelis, A.D., St-Pierre, D.H., Conus, F., et al.
- **Year**: 2007 | **PMID**: 17697862 | **DOI**: 10.1016/j.metabol.2007.03.011
- **Journal**: Metabolism: Clinical and Experimental
- **Sample**: 38 obese women
- **Study Type**: Cross-sectional
- **Key Finding**: Establishes strict metabolic health criteria
- **QS**: 8
- **Related Card**: q-trig-metabolic (supporting)

---

### 6. wang-2023-metabolic
- **Title**: Plasma metabolites of healthy lifestyle in relation to mortality
- **Authors**: Wang, Y., Sun, D., Vendemiale, G., et al.
- **Year**: 2023 | **PMID**: 36599867 | **DOI**: 10.1097/MD.0000000000032759
- **Journal**: Medicine
- **Sample**: >100,000 (4 prospective US cohorts, 20-28 year follow-up)
- **Study Type**: Prospective cohort meta-analysis
- **Key Finding**: Healthy metabolomic signature → 17% ↓ mortality, 25% ↑ longevity
- **QS**: 12
- **Primary Card**: q-score-high

---

### 7. reaven-1988
- **Title**: Banting Lecture 1988: Role of Insulin Resistance
- **Authors**: Reaven, G.M.
- **Year**: 1988 | **PMID**: 3056758 | **DOI**: 10.2337/diab.37.12.1595
- **Journal**: Diabetes
- **Study Type**: Landmark conceptual paper
- **Key Finding**: Proposed "Syndrome X" (metabolic syndrome); insulin resistance central
- **QS**: 13 (LANDMARK)
- **Primary Card**: q-trig-metabolic, q-hdl-metabolic

### 8. glucose-diabetes-collaboration-2011

- **Year**: 2011 | **PMID**: 21443997 | **DOI**: 10.1007/s00125-011-2144-2
- **Journal**: Diabetologia
- **Sample**: >1,000,000 (meta-analysis)
- **Study Type**: Meta-analysis
- **Key Finding**: Fasting glucose linear risk gradient from normal range (no threshold)

## EXISTING STUDIES (4 Total)

### balkau-2014
- **Title**: Predicting Diabetes (DESIR Study)
- **PMID**: 18689695 | **DOI**: 10.2337/dc08-0382
- **Key Finding**: Fasting insulin top quartile → 3× diabetes incidence (6 years)
- **Primary Card**: q-insulin (first study)

### sarwar-2007
- **Title**: Triglycerides and CHD Risk
- **Key Finding**: Triglycerides independently → 72% ↑ CHD risk
- **Related Card**: q-trig-metabolic

### gordon dj-1989
- **Title**: HDL and Cardiovascular Disease
- **PMID**: 2642759 | **DOI**: 10.1161/01.CIR.79.1.8
- **Key Finding**: Each 1 mg/dL HDL → 2-3% ↓ CHD risk
- **Related Card**: q-hdl-metabolic

### knowler-2002
- **Title**: Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP)
- **PMID**: 11832527 | **DOI**: 10.1056/NEJMoa012512
- **Key Finding**: Lifestyle → 58% ↓ diabetes (NNT 6.9)
- **Primary Card**: q-improve-metabolic

## Key Finding Highlights

### Metabolic Syndrome & Mortality
- Metabolic syndrome → **2× CVD risk** (mottillo-2010)
- Metabolic syndrome → **1.5× all-cause mortality** (mottillo-2010)
- MHO → **1.24× mortality** vs normal weight (kramer-2013)

### Fasting Insulin
- Top quartile → **3× diabetes incidence** (balkau-2014)
- Hyperinsulinemia → **1.58× mortality** (malmö-2003)

### Fasting Glucose
- **No threshold** for disease risk (glucose-2011)
- **Linear risk** from normal glucose levels (glucose-2011)

### Metabolic Health
- Only **12.2% Americans** are metabolically healthy (araujo-2019)
- Healthy metabolomic signature → **17% ↓ mortality** (wang-2023)
- Healthy signature → **25% ↑ longevity** (wang-2023)

### Intervention
- Lifestyle intervention → **58% ↓ diabetes** (knowler-2002)
