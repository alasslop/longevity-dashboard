Scientific Evidence

The 5 nutrition principles are based on meta-analyses and systematic reviews involving millions of participants.

Scientific Consensus

The True Health Initiative brought together 500+ experts from 40+ countries who agreed: "The healthiest diet is one generally comprised mostly of minimally processed plants."

1
Eat at least 3 servings of vegetables daily
13% lower mortality
Wang et al. 2021 - Circulation
108,735 participants | 30-year follow-up | 33,898 deaths
Key finding: 5 servings/day vs 2 servings/day = 13% lower all-cause mortality. Leafy greens and cruciferous vegetables showed strongest benefits. Starchy vegetables (potatoes, corn) showed NO mortality benefit.
View on PubMed →
Aune et al. 2017 - Meta-Analysis
95 prospective studies | ~2 million participants
Key finding: Per 200g vegetables/day: 10-15% mortality reduction. Vegetables showed STRONGER associations with reduced cancer and CVD mortality than fruits.

2
Home-cooked meals from whole ingredients
15% lower mortality
BMJ Umbrella Review 2024
45 pooled analyses | 9.9 million participants
Key finding: Higher ultra-processed food (UPF) intake associated with 32 adverse health outcomes including +15% all-cause mortality, +50% CVD mortality, increased cancer, diabetes, and mental health disorders.
View on PubMed →
Hall NIH RCT 2019 - Cell Metabolism
Randomized controlled trial | Inpatient metabolic ward
Key finding: UPF diet caused +508 kcal/day consumed vs whole foods diet (matched for calories, protein, fat, carbs, fiber). UPF causes passive overeating through faster eating and bypassed satiety signals.
View on PubMed →
3
Include healthy fats regularly
30% less CVD
PREDIMED Trial 2018 - NEJM
7,447 participants | Randomized controlled trial | 4.8 years
Key finding: Mediterranean diet with extra virgin olive oil (50g/day) or nuts (30g/day) = 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events vs low-fat control diet. Benefit independent of weight loss.
View on PubMed →
4
Avoid trans fats and limit processed meats
34% mortality reduction
de Souza et al. 2015 - BMJ Meta-Analysis
Systematic review of observational studies
Key finding: Trans fat intake associated with +34% all-cause mortality, +28% CHD mortality, +21% CHD events per 2% of energy from trans fats. Trans fats show STRONGEST evidence for harm among all dietary fats.
View on PubMed →
AHA Presidential Advisory 2017
American Heart Association scientific statement
Key finding: Industrial trans fats consistently associated with CHD death. Recommendation: Eliminate partially hydrogenated oils from diet completely.
View on AHA →
5
Moderate alcohol or none at all
Linear dose-response
GBD 2016 - Lancet Global Study
195 countries | 28 million people | 694 data sources
Key finding: Zero alcohol = lowest risk for all-cause mortality. NO safe level of alcohol identified. Previous "J-curve" (protective effect of moderate drinking) was methodological artifact.
View on PubMed →
Wood et al. 2018 - Lancet
83 prospective studies | 599,912 current drinkers
Key finding: >7 drinks/week: mortality increases. 14 drinks/week: -1 to -2 years life expectancy. 25 drinks/week: -4 to -5 years life expectancy. Linear dose-response with no safe threshold.
View on PubMed →
